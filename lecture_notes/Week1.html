Course Overview

What is this class?

This is a 1 hour seminar acting as an introduction to the major, interesting issues and related research, and the avenues available to students who attain a degree in that major.

We will cover:

  • Research and academic ethics.
  • Career pathways.
  • Study habits.
  • The application of the major to current issues and careers.

Icebreaker

The "Personal Dataset" 🧊

We have a great small group today. Let's get to know each other.

Go around the room and share:

  1. Your Name & Hometown
  2. The Data Question: If you could magically have a spreadsheet containing one specific statistic about your entire life so far, what would it be? (Example: how many miles you've scrolled on TikTok, gallons of boba consumed, and so on.)

Data Science is all about finding the stories hidden in everyday information.

Homework 1

Let's form groups 🤝

Today's primary goal is introductions, icebreakers, small groups, and project assignments.

Instructions

Get into groups of 3 to 4 people.

Due Week 2

Each group proposes two topics to investigate and present in weeks 9 and 10.

Presentation Format

The topic can be any scientific topic the group wants to learn more about through a 15 minute presentation and Q&A, with 10 minutes for the presentation and 5 minutes for questions and answers.

Note: For Homework 5, groups will identify a topic they are curious about, investigate it, and present it to the class.
